Tom Lutz

President

 

Founder

Tom brings to the Board a wealth of educational and recreational experience, as well as a combined time in the Galapagos Islands of over one year. Graduating with a Master of Science degree in Zoology, he taught Biology, Earth Science, and Marine Biology for over 40 years in the public schools of Arizona. His teaching experience also includes nearly 32 years with the Arizona Army National Guard, retiring as the Director of Non-commissioned Officer education in 2002, with the rank of Command Sergeant Major. Establishing the Galapagos Marine Ecology course for the University of Arizona in 2003 and continuing to teach a continuing education course for the University of San Diego in the Galapagos Islands, Mr. Lutz continues to be actively involved in Galapagos. He has also sponsored two Galapagos students as exchange students to the United Stated for a period of nearly 6 years, along with bringing several other students from Galapagos to visit Arizona and Southern California since 2006. Serving as a volunteer leader in Young Life for nearly 25 years, Tom has maintained a strong commitment to students of the United States and the Galapagos Islands. He has also worked in non-profit foundations since 2003.

 

Sebastian Ramirez

Secretary

 

Secretary

Sebastian is currently studying at the University of Arizona majoring in economics after studying architecture at the University of San Francisco, Quito, Ecuador. He brings to the Foundation vast experience with the people of the Galapagos Islands, where he is a resident. As an international student, Sebastian attended Glendale Community College in Glendale, Arizona, after graduating from Westwind Preparatory Academy, Phoenix, Arizona. In the Galapagos Islands, Sebastian was a student leader and fulfilled over 200 hours of volunteer work with the Galapagos National Park. He participated in the Galapagos Marine Ecology program of the University of Arizona as a student and co-coordinator. His experience as a high school exchange student, an international college student, his recent studies in sustainable resources, and his devotion for improving the lives of the Galapagos people make him a vital asset to the Foundation.
